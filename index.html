Fall damage is so underdone in Rpgs!

Brian Moore

GM: “Tsurugi the ninja is falling at a rate of speed, he has some armor on. He will impact the ground in three seconds”. Player: “Ugh. . . . How much damage does he take?” Gm: “I don’t know there really aren’t rules for this situation” Player: “So then My character takes no damage right?” (Har har so original!). This situation has come up many times in my experiences gaming and the rules have never been written well for most circumstances. You may have come across some rules involving fall damage but I have never seen rules for a heavy weight falling on someone. Nor have I seen rules in any rpg that deals with a heavy object hitting someone from the side(like a pendulum trap). You have to account for the lack of weight but potentially increased momentum. Imagine there is a ten foot pole, it weighs. . .say 1,000lbs and is perfectly cylindrical and has a diameter of 3 feet. We need to figure out how this will affect our players when we decide to use that against them. We (as gm’s) have the option to just assign damage to situations like this but, I don’t think that is very fair in the long run. There are times when the same trap or fall would do minimal damage in a circumstance and at other times could potentially kill. I find that it is all how you are interpreting the scene that day. Using a specialized “game-math” for mechanics like this, your player’s won’t try to argue their way out of fall damage or the kinetic force of an object again! I have a very special brand of mathematics for Doa. Though, I wouldn’t recommend trying to adapt it to your game. I would recommend creating your own unique brand of math for whatever system you are running. Making any shortcomings in their rule system less apparent and keeping the same blind spots you never saw, out of sight. Use whatever math they have available to base your math on and consider adding absolute limits. I personally recommend them for almost all circumstances but, there are exceptions to every rule! Absolute limits means things like terminal velocity, the coldest the planet can get, or how hot lava is. Once you start to figure those things out you will begin to see how to apply some math to improve your games. I also say keep it as simple as your players will be happy with. Making overly complex rules slows down the gaming and can become a lot like homework! Nobody wants that. Keep it easy, fluid and if you can: elegant. I hope these posts are not too confusing for some since they are meant for tabletop role players. I know it is short and dives straight into the topic. This is a learning experience for me and I hope for you as well!
